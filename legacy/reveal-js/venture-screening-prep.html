Narrative Spine 0 / setup

Answer the questionnaire as one story.

Need

Who hurts, how badly, and why now?

Name the beachhead customer, the workflow failure, the measurable cost, and the trigger that makes the problem urgent.

Edge

What can you do that the current state cannot?

Contrast the existing workaround with your technical advance, then connect that advance to a testable performance claim.

Execution

What proof and capital unlock the next stage?

Show TRL, validation evidence, IP posture, team fit, funding allocation, milestones, and institutional obligations.

Do not answer as ten disconnected essays. Tie every answer back to this spine: customer pain, technical edge, proof plan, and value capture.
Question 1 customer segment

What specific customer segment or end-user problem are you hoping to address?

Answer shape

Specific user, specific job, specific pain.

For [beachhead user] doing [job], the current [workflow] fails because [constraint], causing [measurable loss].
UserWho has budget, urgency, and a repeated workflow?
PainWhat is slow, risky, expensive, inaccurate, or impossible?
CostWhat metric exposes the pain: time, waste, risk, revenue, compliance?
TriggerWhy does this segment need a new answer now?
Prepare one beachhead segment, not a broad market label. If you have multiple users, name the end user and the economic buyer separately.
Question 2 technology advance

Can you provide a brief overview of the technology and how it advances beyond the current state of the art?

Layer Current state Your advance Proof signal
Core mechanism What today's best solution can and cannot do. The technical principle that changes the limit. Benchmark, experiment, or validation result.
System design Where integration, cost, latency, reliability, or scale breaks. The architecture that removes the bottleneck. Prototype metric or credible engineering model.
User outcome The old workflow's performance ceiling. The new capability visible to the customer. Before-after task, cost, or risk reduction.
Avoid jargon-first explanations. Start with the capability, name the technical breakthrough, and finish with the evidence you can show or still need to generate.

Beachhead

Value capture

Show the business model as a consequence of the workflow.

  • Buyer and procurement path
  • Pricing anchor tied to customer value
  • Sales motion: pilot, deployment, expansion
  • Defensibility: data, workflow lock-in, integration, IP
If market sizing is uncertain, say so cleanly. A credible wedge plus a rational value capture model is stronger than inflated TAM arithmetic.

Question 7 team

Founder-market fit

Technical depth

Gap plan

Connect each person's expertise to a near-term risk. A reviewer is asking whether the team can retire the risks implied by the milestones.

Question 9 12-18 months

What are the next three to five critical milestones for the venture over the coming 12-18 months?

0-3 mo

Define proof

Lock requirements, success metrics, baseline, and test plan.

3-6 mo

Build prototype

Retire the hardest technical uncertainty with a working system.

6-9 mo

Validate user value

Run pilot or controlled study with target users and buying signals.

9-12 mo

Package deployment

Document integration, safety, reliability, cost, and workflow fit.

12-18 mo

Scale decision

Use evidence to choose raise, license, paid pilots, or first sales.

Each milestone should have a deliverable, owner, date range, budget dependency, and success metric. Avoid vague milestones like "continue development."
Question 10 affiliation

Are you affiliated with a university, research institution, or company that may have rights or obligations tied to the technology?

Disclosure map

Separate facts from unresolved obligations.

  • Employment, student, faculty, contractor, or lab status
  • Use of institution facilities, funds, data, code, or materials
  • Invention disclosure, sponsored research, or grant terms
  • Conflict-of-interest and conflict-of-commitment review
  • License path, option path, or clean-room development path
Recommended answer tone

Transparent, bounded, and action-oriented.

State known obligations, what has been disclosed, who is reviewing it, and what path clears the venture to commercialize.

"We have identified the relevant institutional rights questions and are working through [office/counsel/process] before executing commercial agreements."
This is a trust question. Do not minimize obligations. Reviewers want to know that rights will not block customer pilots, financing, or commercialization.
Use this slide as the opening answer. Once this is crisp, the ten detailed questions become supporting evidence rather than a scramble.
Readiness Check before the call

Bring proof, not polish alone.

Three numbers

Problem cost, performance delta, and funding-to-milestone amount.

Three artifacts

Prototype evidence, customer synthesis, and milestone budget.

Three risks

Technical, market, and rights/IP risk with mitigation owner.

The strongest screening answer is specific where evidence exists and explicit where proof is still being built.
Final rehearsal: answer every question in under 60 seconds, then prepare one deeper follow-up per question in case the screener asks for detail.
